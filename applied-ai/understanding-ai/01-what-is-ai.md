You've probably heard a lot of terms thrown around: Artificial Intelligence, Machine Learning, Deep Learning, Generative AI. They all mean different things, but they're related. Understanding where each one sits helps make sense of the tools you'll work with throughout this course.

## The Categories

**Artificial Intelligence** is the broadest term. It covers any technique that allows a machine to simulate intelligent behavior. That includes hand-coded rule systems, search algorithms, and the most advanced neural networks. A game enemy navigating a maze is AI. So is ChatGPT.

**Machine Learning** is a subfield of AI where computers learn patterns from data. Instead of a programmer writing every rule, you give the system examples and it figures out the patterns on its own. Predicting house prices from historical sales data, flagging spam emails, recommending videos - these are all ML tasks.

**Neural Networks** are a specific ML technique inspired by how the brain processes information. They use layers of interconnected nodes to model complex patterns. They're what make image recognition, speech recognition, and text generation work at scale.

**Generative AI** uses deep neural networks to create new content: text, images, code, audio, video. When you use ChatGPT or DALL-E, you're using generative AI.

## How They Relate

Here's the part that trips people up: these aren't separate buckets. They nest inside each other. Generative AI is also neural networks. Neural networks are also machine learning. Machine learning is also AI. A single technology can be accurately described by multiple labels at once.
