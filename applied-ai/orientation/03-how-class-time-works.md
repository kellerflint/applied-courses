## In Class: Guided Activities

Class time is spent on guided partner activities. You and a partner work through interactive demos and exercises together, step by step, with discussion questions and prompts along the way. Sometimes there's a short knowledge check at the start. Later in the quarter there will be studio time for the final project.

## Why This Format

Active learning beats passive listening. Applying material yourself and explaining it to a partner is more effective than watching someone else explain it. A written walkthrough you can re-read and reference anytime is also more useful than a live lecture delivered once.

What actually works is applying the material and explaining it to others. You learn more from walking your partner through a concept than from watching anyone describe it on a projector. Class time is most valuable when you're active, talking, experimenting, and thinking out loud.

## Where I Am

I'm usually working on future content at the back of the room, and I wander around the room periodically. Flag me down or come find me when you have a question. I'd much rather help you one-on-one when you're actually stuck than talk at the whole room simultaneously. That's when I can actually sit down with you, look at what you have, and help you get unstuck fast.

**Don't stay stuck for more than 10-15 minutes.** That's the hard limit. If you've been spinning on something for that long, stop and come get me. I can usually spot the issue in a couple of minutes. Struggling in silence for half an hour when I can unblock you in just a few minutes is a bad use of your time.

## How to Get the Most Out of Class

Do the async material. Come in ready to work. Talk to your partner constantly. Ask each other questions out loud. The lessons have prompts, reflections, and discussion questions throughout to help scaffold this. Take them seriously.

If one of you doesn't understand something, slow down and work through it together before moving on. Teaching is really good for learning. Not understanding something is a huge gift to your partner. It gives them the opportunity to explain it.

The goal is to finish the activity having understood it. Take your time. Learn the material deeply.
