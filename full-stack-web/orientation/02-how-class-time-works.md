## Before Class: Do the Async Material

All initial instruction comes through async material: readings, videos, and exercises. These are your lectures. Complete them before you show up.

I will assume you've had first exposure to everything we're working on. Arriving without the async material done means you won't be able to participate effectively. You'll be trying to absorb new concepts while also trying to build something with a partner who's already past that point.

## In Class: Pair Programming Walkthroughs

Class time is spent on guided pair programming. You and a partner build something together, step by step, working through discussion questions and activities along the way. Sometimes there's a short knowledge check at the start. Later in the quarter there will be studio time for the final project.

## Why This Format

Active learning beats passive listening. Applying material yourself and explaining it to a partner is more effective than watching someone else explain it. A written walkthrough you can re-read and reference anytime is also more useful than a live lecture delivered once.

You learn more from struggling with problems directly and walking your partner through code than from just watching someone talk or copying code from a projector. Class time is most valuable when you're engaged, talking, building, thinking, and actively solving problems.

## Where I Am

I'm usually working on future content at the back of the room, and I wander around the room periodically. Sometimes I might stop by to check on your progress or ask questions. Please feel free to flag me down or come find me when you have a question. I'd much rather help you one-on-one when you're actually stuck than talk at the whole room simultaneously. That's when I can actually sit down with you, look at what you have, and help you get unstuck fast.

**Don't stay stuck for more than 10-15 minutes.** That's the hard limit. If you've been spinning on something for that long, stop and come get me. I can usually spot the issue in a couple of minutes. Initially attempting to solve things yourself is great, but struggling in silence on the same thing for half an hour when I can unblock you in just a few minutes is a bad use of your time.

## How to Get the Most Out of Class

Do the async material. Come in ready. Talk to your partner constantly. Ask each other questions and answer out loud. Think about those answers and how complete/incomplete they are. The lessons have prompts, reflections, and discussion questions throughout to help scaffold this. Take them seriously.

If one of you doesn't understand something, slow down and work through it together before moving on. Teaching something is probably the single best way to learn it deeply. Not understsanding a topic can feel bad, but it is actually a huge gift to your partner. It gives them the opportunity to practice explaining it.

The goal is to finish walkthroughs having understood them. Take your time. Learn the material deeply.
