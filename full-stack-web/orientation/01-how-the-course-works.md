This course focuses on React. You'll build real projects with it throughout the quarter. The "Full Stack" in the course name refers to the capstone: you'll be building the frontend here while your Algorithms (334) class builds the backend for the same project. Both sides need to work together.

## What You'll Be Doing in Class

Most class time is spent pair programming through guided walkthroughs. You'll build projects with a partner, step by step, with discussion and questions woven in. Occasionally there will be a short quiz or knowledge check at the start of class. Later in the quarter there will be studio time to work on the final project.

All initial instruction is delivered async through readings, videos, and interactive exercises. These are your lectures. You need to complete them before you come to class.

## Grading

This course uses completion-based grading. Every assignment is worth exactly 1 point. If you more or less did it, you get full credit. If you more or less didn't, you get zero.

Everything counts equally:

- Pair programs
- Homework assignments (Scrimba)
- Project milestones
- In-person attendance and participation (each class day is 1 point)

Your lowest 3 scores for attendance are automatically dropped, so you can miss up to 3 days without penalty. This should cover all normal illness and life events. You do not need to notify me if you are going to miss a class.

If some larger medical concern or similar comes up that will result in missing mutliple weeks, get in touch with me and we'll discuss options going forward.

## Attendance

Attendance is typically checked within the first 15 minutes of class, starting in week 2. Arriving late earns half credit for that day.

Being late is a problem beyond the grade. It means missing instructions, disrupting the room while you find your partner, and making things harder for everyone around you. Develop the habit of arriving **5 minutes early to everything**, not just this class. Early arrival signals reliability and professionalism. People notice. It will serve you in every job and every team you work on.

## Late Work

You have 24 hours after the due date to submit late work and still get full credit. After that, late submissions receive a 50% penalty, but you can still turn them in.

If you need an extension, ask before 5pm on the day the assignment is due. The default extension is 3 days. If you need more time than that, say so when you reach out. Try to use extensions sparingly. The material builds on itself, and falling behind makes everything harder.

## Midterm and Final Reviews

You'll meet with me twice: once at midterm and once at finals. We'll sit down, open your code, and walk through it together. I'll ask you to explain what you wrote and why you made the choices you did.

You need to be able to answer. Write code you understand well enough to explain from scratch. If you can't explain it, that's a signal to go back and understand it better.

## The Most Valuable Skill in This Class

Being able to talk about code clearly and confidently matters more than most people will ever tell you. You have to explain it out loud to another person, describe what something does, justify a design decision, and ask precise questions when you're stuck.

That skill is noticed in every interview, every code review, and every team you'll work on. This class is structured to build it. Take that part seriously.
